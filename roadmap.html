About SBDC

The NorCal Small Business Development Center (SBDC) is part of a national network that provides no-cost business consulting and low-cost training to help entrepreneurs start, grow, and succeed. We are one of the pillars of America's small business support ecosystem, funded in part through a cooperative agreement with the U.S. Small Business Administration.

AI Pillar Roadmap

Our phased approach to bringing AI tools and resources to the SBDC network. We're walking before we run — building foundational skills first, then scaling to more advanced tools.

Our Approach

The goal is to get advisors comfortable using AI — and thinking "I'm going to use AI" instead of doing everything manually. We start with low-barrier, beginner-friendly tools that create psychological safety, then build toward more powerful custom solutions as the network gains confidence and we better understand what advisors actually need.

In Progress

Foundation & Adoption

Prompt Library

A click-and-copy platform that gives advisors safe, pointed prompt templates to start with. Low barrier and beginner-friendly. Shared across the network with lunch-and-learn demos on how prompts work in advising practice.

Live now

Needs Assessment

Internal training paired with discovery work to figure out what advisors actually need. We can't build the right tools without that input. Survey data and direct feedback shape everything that follows.

In progress

Access & Office Hours

A calendar of Zoom training sessions and drop-in office hours so resources are accessible to the full network — not just the early adopters. Open to all advisors regardless of skill level.

Scheduling

NotebookLM — Network Catalog

Catalog the entire network's processes, training resources, and compliance documentation into AI-powered notebooks. This foundational knowledge base directly feeds the design of custom GPTs later — the better we understand our own workflows now, the better tools we build next.

Starting now
Next Phase

Scale & Customize

ChatGPT Business

Once budget and procurement are in place, we secure a team license and start building custom GPTs for the network — both advising-facing tools for client work and internal tools for staff operations.

Pending procurement

Custom GPTs

Purpose-built GPTs for complex, multi-step advising processes. Built on the network catalog from NotebookLM, these require real data and careful design — more care, testing, and auditing than the prompt library. The payoff is significant for structured workflows.

Informed by Phase 1 catalog

How We'll Measure Success

Simple, trackable indicators to know if our AI initiatives are landing. These help us iterate and justify continued investment to network leadership.

Short-Term (0–3 months)

Prompt Library engagement
Page visits, unique users, and copy-to-clipboard actions per week
Office hours attendance
Number of advisors attending Zoom sessions and repeat attendance rate
First-use adoption
% of network advisors who have tried at least one AI prompt with a real client
NotebookLM coverage
Number of centers and processes cataloged into the network knowledge base

Long-Term (3–12 months)

Regular AI usage rate
% of advisors reporting weekly AI use in client work (target: 50%+)
Custom GPT adoption
Number of active custom GPTs in use and advisor satisfaction scores
Time savings
Self-reported hours saved per advisor per month on routine tasks
Network confidence score
Follow-up survey measuring advisor comfort and confidence with AI tools vs. baseline
